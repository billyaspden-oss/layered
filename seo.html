Layered SEO · Built to compound

Four layers of SEO,
each one building on the last.

A complete SEO package designed for local businesses in Cumbria and the Lake District. We don't sell bolt-ons or tricks — we build the stack from the foundation up, so every month's work compounds the one before.

Why layered? Most SEO agencies sell you one tactic at a time — a fresh batch of blog posts, a directory submission package, a tweak to your titles. None of it sticks because the layer underneath isn't there. We build the whole stack.

How it works: Each layer takes 4–6 weeks to land and continues paying off afterwards. You don't pay for layer 02 until layer 01 is doing its job — and you can stop after any layer if you've reached the visibility you need.

01
— Foundation

Technical SEO

If Google can't crawl, index, and trust the site, nothing above this layer matters. We start here every time.

  • Crawlability audit — robots, sitemaps, indexation issues, bot-blocking misconfigurations
  • Core Web Vitals — page speed, mobile usability, layout stability
  • Structured data — schema markup for local business, services, reviews, FAQ
  • Site architecture — clean URLs, internal linking, canonical & redirect hygiene
02
— Local

Google Business Profile & map

For local trade, this is the highest-leverage layer in the stack. We treat it as a full mini-website.

  • GBP optimisation — categories, services, attributes, photos, opening hours, products
  • Map-pack targeting — local search visibility for "near me" and town-specific queries
  • Citations & NAP consistency — claim & standardise listings across Yell, Bing, Apple Maps, sector directories
  • Local schema — structured data so Google understands service area, hours, certifications
03
— On-page

Service & location pages

The pages that turn searches into enquiries — written for humans, structured for Google.

  • Keyword-targeted service pages — one page per high-intent search ("fencing contractor Kendal")
  • Location pages — for every town you serve, written with local context, not boilerplate
  • FAQ & how-to content — long-tail questions that catch buyers earlier in the funnel
  • Titles, meta & headings — rewritten across the site to align with search intent
04
— Authority

Reputation & reach

The slow-burn layer. Reviews, content, and links that compound over months and years.

  • Review automation — every completed job triggers a Google review request
  • Content cadence — one anchor article per month around buyer-intent keywords
  • Local backlinks — partnerships with non-competing local businesses and trade bodies
  • Press & directories — pitches to local press, association listings, sector directories
